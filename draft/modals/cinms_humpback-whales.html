Humpback whales in CINMS

Sights & Sounds

Humpback whales Megaptera novaeangliae, such as this one breaching in Channel Islands National Marine Sanctuary, hunt for anchovies, sardines and other prey in sanctuary waters. Photo: Barbara LaCorte NOAA/CINMS

Humpback whales make a variety of sounds including moans, cries, groans and clicks. This humpback whale call produced between 100 to 900 Hz was recorded in CINMS.

Time series

Humpback whale calls were detected at listening station CI05 in CINMS during most weeks for which we have recordings. Recordings are not available during most of February, March and two weeks during July-August of 2019. Humpback whale calls were detected less often in the summer and more often in the winter and spring.

Monthly Patterns

Humpback whale were heard at listening station CI05 in CINMS during all months for which we have recordings; recordings were not available during most of February and March 2019. Humpback whale calls were detected more often in the spring and in the fall October, November and December.
