Vessels

Sights & Sounds

A variety of small to very large vessels are common in Olympic Coast sanctuary. This is a photo of the sanctuary’s 50 foot high-speed catamaran R/V Storm Petrel, which supports science and research activities in and around santuary waters. Photo: Kevin Grant/NOAA OCNMS

Propulsion systems, such as spinning propeller blades, and the flow of water over the ship’s hull are the main sources of the underwater noises made by large commerical ships. These sounds tend to be louder at higher ship speeds. This low frequency vessel noise was recorded in 2019 from the listening station in the northeast corner of the sanctuary, near the mouth of Straight of Juan de Fuca at Neah Bay. This area is protected habitat for both humpback whales and Southern Resident killer whales.
