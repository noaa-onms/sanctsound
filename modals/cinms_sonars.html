Sonars

Sights & Sounds

Hull-mounted active sonars support Naval operations offshore. This image of a Naval ship out of the water shows an example of a sonar dome (front silver part of hull) that transmits these signals. Photo: Office of U.S. Navy

Naval sonar uses short bursts of sound to detect objects under water. This low frequency active sonar was recorded around 1,000 Hertz east of Anacapa Island in Channel Islands sanctuary in 2018.

Time series

Sonar was detected nearly weekly November-February in 2018-2019 and 2019-2020 north of Santa Rosa Island in Channel Islands santuary, and rarely ever outside of those months. Although we don’t have data at this site from fall 2019, no sonar was detected in winter months of 2020.

Monthly Patterns

During this project, sonar was almost exclusively detected November-February north of Santa Rosa Island in Channel Islands sanctuary and hardly ever in other months, however variation around sonar detections (wider gray band around the black line representing average) were large.

Hourly Patterns

With so few detections, an hourly sonar use pattern isn’t clear north of Santa Rosa Island in Channel Islands sanctuary in this three year time-series.
